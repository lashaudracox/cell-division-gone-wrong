LESSON 1
Interactive Activities: Card Sort

📖 How This Works

You'll complete two interactive activities to practice what you learned in Lesson 1:

  1. Activity 1: Phase Sequencer — Arrange cards to show the correct order of the cell cycle phases
  2. Activity 2: Checkpoint Matcher — Match checkpoints to what they check for

For each activity, check your work when you're done. Read the feedback carefully to see if you need to try again.

Activity 1: Phase Sequencer

Drag the phase cards into the correct order

On the left are cards describing what happens during each phase. On the right, arrange them in the correct order from first to last. Drag cards from left to right.

Why This Order Matters

Now that you've arranged the phases in order, explain: Why can't the cell divide before copying DNA? What would happen if phases happened out of order?

Activity 2: Checkpoint Matcher

Match checkpoints to what they check for

Click a checkpoint on the left, then click a matching description on the right to connect them. A line will appear showing your match.

✓ What They Check

Checks if chromosomes are attached to spindle fibers

Why Three Checkpoints?

Each checkpoint checks for something different at a different time. Why is it important to have all three instead of just one checkpoint? What would be missing if we only had the Spindle Assembly Checkpoint?

💭 Discussion Questions

Discuss these with a partner or in class:

  1. What could go wrong if the S phase checkpoint didn't exist? Trace through what would happen to a cell with small DNA damage.
  2. Why do you think the Spindle Assembly Checkpoint is called the "last chance"? What makes it different from the other two?
  3. Bridge to Next Lesson: We've learned that checkpoints PREVENT problems. In Lesson 2, you'll learn about the proteins that actually DO the checking (like p53). Why do you think identifying which protein is broken would be important for treating cancer?
