Figure 5.1: Cancer develops through the multi-hit hypothesis — the gradual accumulation of multiple checkpoint failures over time. A single mutation rarely causes cancer because the other checkpoints can usually catch the problem (Stage 2). But as more mutations accumulate and more checkpoints fail (Stages 3 and 4), cells lose the ability to stop dividing. The green ✓ marks represent checkpoints still working; red ✗ marks represent checkpoints that have been damaged by mutations. This process typically takes 10–20 years, which is why cancer risk increases with age.
