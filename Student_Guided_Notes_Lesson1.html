Section 4: Cancer Connection

Understand: Why Cancer Needs Multiple Failures

Reflection #1: Single vs. Multiple Failures

Why is ONE checkpoint failure usually not enough to cause cancer, but MULTIPLE failures over time can?

Reflection #2: The Domino Effect

Imagine a cell with damaged DNA:

• If only the G1/S checkpoint fails, what could happen next?

• If that same cell then has a G2/M checkpoint failure, what happens?

• Why does this accumulation of failures over many cell divisions matter for cancer?

Reflection #3: Connecting to St. Jude Research

St. Jude researchers study which proteins are broken in cancer cells. Based on what you learned, why would identifying which checkpoint failed be important for treating cancer?
