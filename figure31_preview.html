Figure 3.1: The three major checkpoints function as decision gates that control progression through the cell cycle. Each gate verifies specific conditions before allowing the cell to continue. If the cell passes (✓), it moves forward to the next phase. If it fails (✗), the cycle pauses so the cell can repair the problem, complete the missing step, or — if the damage is severe — undergo programmed cell death (apoptosis) to protect the organism. The mini cell-cycle wheel in each panel shows where that checkpoint sits in the overall cycle.
