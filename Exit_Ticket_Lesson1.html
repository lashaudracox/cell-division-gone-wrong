Question 1 of 3 · Multiple Choice
A cell reaches the end of G2 with several unrepaired double-strand DNA breaks. Under normal conditions, what happens?
AThe cell proceeds into mitosis — DNA breaks can be repaired during M phase
BATM/ATR kinase detects the breaks and the G2/M checkpoint halts the cycle until repair is complete
CThe G1/S checkpoint activates and sends the cell back to G1 to redo replication
Dp53 detects the breaks and forces the cell to skip directly to cytokinesis
✓ Correct. ATM/ATR is the G2/M checkpoint sensor — it detects double-strand breaks after replication and halts the cell cycle until repair is possible.
The G2/M checkpoint — using ATM/ATR kinase — monitors DNA integrity after replication. p53 operates earlier, at G1/S.
Question 2 of 3 · Multiple Choice
What is the function of MAD2, and during which phase of mitosis does it operate?
AMAD2 repairs double-strand DNA breaks during prophase
BMAD2 signals the cell to wait during metaphase if any chromosome is not properly attached to spindle fibers
CMAD2 activates apoptosis in G1 when DNA damage is detected by p53
DMAD2 initiates cytokinesis by triggering the contractile ring during telophase
✓ Correct. MAD2 is the Spindle Assembly Checkpoint sensor — it operates during metaphase to ensure all chromosomes are attached before anaphase begins.
MAD2 is the key protein of the Spindle Assembly Checkpoint, which operates during metaphase. It blocks anaphase until every chromosome is attached to spindle fibers at both kinetochores.
